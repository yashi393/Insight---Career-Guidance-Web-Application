Robotics

Robotics combines mechanical engineering, electronics, and computer science to design, build, and operate robots for industrial, medical, and consumer applications. Modern robotics integrates AI, computer vision, sensors, and automation to perform complex tasks in dynamic environments.

Difficulty
Advanced
Typical time to job-readiness
6 - 12 months (foundations) • 2–4 years (specialist)

Skills Required

Robotics Programming

Python, C++, ROS, robot kinematics, control algorithms.

Electronics & Sensors

Embedded systems, microcontrollers, actuators, LiDAR, IMU, cameras.

Computer Vision & AI

Image processing, object detection, SLAM, deep learning for perception.

Simulation & Testing

Gazebo, V-REP, MATLAB, ROS simulation, real-world validation.

Job roles

Robotics Engineer

Design & build robots.

Develop robot hardware, control systems, and software integration.

Perception / CV Engineer

Computer vision & AI.

Implement vision, sensor fusion, and perception algorithms.

Automation / Industrial Engineer

Factory automation.

Deploy industrial robots for assembly, quality inspection, and logistics.

Robotics Researcher

R&D & innovation.

Work on novel robotics algorithms, human-robot interaction, swarm robotics.

Embedded Systems Engineer

Firmware & sensors.

Develop embedded software for microcontrollers and real-time control.

Real-world starter projects

Autonomous Robot Navigation

Implement SLAM and path planning for a mobile robot using ROS.

Sensor Integration

Connect IMU, LiDAR, and camera to a robot and fuse data for perception.

Robotic Arm Object Picking

Use computer vision and AI to detect and pick objects with a robotic arm.

Robot Simulation in Gazebo

Simulate a complete robot in Gazebo before deploying to hardware.

Pros & Cons

Pros

• Cutting-edge domain combining AI, hardware, and automation

• High demand in research, industrial automation, and AI labs

• Opportunities to work on humanoids, drones, and autonomous vehicles

• Interdisciplinary skillset valued globally

Cons

• Requires broad skillset (mechanical, electronics, programming)

• Hardware setup and testing can be expensive and time-consuming

• Some roles require advanced degrees or research experience

• Debugging and integration can be complex and iterative
